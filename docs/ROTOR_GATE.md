## 2. The Rotor Gate: Replacing AND/OR with Resonance

Traditional computers rely on Logic Gates (AND, OR, XOR) built from transistors. These gates operate on Real numbers (voltage levels representing 1 and 0). They are fundamentally particles crashing into each other to yield a rigid, binary outcome.

The **Rotor Gate**, as implemented in our Proof of Concept (`rotor_gate_poc.py`), discards binary states in favor of **Wave Interference**:
- **Inputs:** Two complex waveforms representing distinct data streams or concepts.
- **The Operation:** Superposition (the addition of complex vectors).

### Constructive Interference (Yang-gak / 양각)
When the two input waves share the same phase (their imaginary components align), they resonate. The output wave has an amplified magnitude.
- **Meaning:** "These states are in agreement." The system naturally reinforces the signal, creating a powerful flow of energy. It is not $1 + 1 = 2$; it is $1 + 1 = \text{Resonance}$.

### Destructive Interference (Eum-gak / 음각) and The Void
When the two input waves have opposite phases (shifted by 180 degrees / $\pi$ radians), they cancel each other out completely.
- **Meaning:** "These states are opposed." The resulting magnitude is zero, but this zero is not an empty absence. It is **"The Void"**—a state of perfect, tense equilibrium. The underlying waves are still present and spinning, but their macroscopic effect is perfectly balanced. It is the ultimate calm achieved through opposing forces.
